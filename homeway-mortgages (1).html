FAQ

Common Questions

Can I buy Dubai property without living there? +
Yes, absolutely. Dubai's freehold zones allow full foreign ownership. You do not need a UAE visa or residency to purchase property. We handle all paperwork remotely.
How much down payment do I need? +
For non-residents, the minimum down payment is 20% for properties up to AED 5 million. This is a one-time payment. For properties above AED 5M, it's 30%. We help you explore off-plan options with even lower entry points.
Will the rental income actually cover my EMI? +
In most cases, yes. Dubai's average rental yield is 6–10% annually, while mortgage rates typically result in an EMI equivalent to 5–7% of property value. We only recommend properties where rent covers or exceeds the EMI. Use our calculator to see your numbers.
What documents do I need as an international buyer? +
Typically: valid passport, 6 months bank statements, proof of income/salary (or business ownership documents), and a UAE bank account (we help you open one). The exact requirements vary by bank and nationality.
Do I get a Dubai visa with property purchase? +
Yes! Purchasing a property worth AED 750,000+ qualifies you for a 2-year investor visa. Properties worth AED 2M+ qualify for a 10-year Golden Visa. This is a major benefit for global investors.
What are the costs beyond the purchase price? +
Dubai Land Department fee (4%), Dubai Mortgage Registration (0.25% of loan), property valuation fee (~AED 2,500), and agent commission (2%). There's NO annual property tax, no capital gains tax, and no income tax on rental income.
